Challenge

The National Debt Management Center (NDMC) needed to communicate the benefits of Sukuk instruments to the Saudi public—a sophisticated financial concept requiring explanation through accessible visual narrative. The challenge: make complex government financial instruments resonate with mass audiences while building trust and driving subscription requests.

The Problem
  • Complex financial instrument difficult to communicate
  • Need to reach mass audience (not just finance sector)
  • Building trust in governmental financial products
  • Measurable target: drive savings requests
  • High-stakes governmental credibility at stake
The Solution
  • Narrative-driven visual storytelling (benefits-focused)
  • Stripped-down design emphasizing clarity
  • Multi-channel campaign (broadcast, digital, print)
  • Trust-building through official, professional aesthetic
  • Clear call-to-action across all touchpoints

Creative Execution

Directed 15+ hero assets across broadcast, digital, and print channels. Each asset spoke to different audience segments while maintaining unified messaging around financial security and national investment.

Strategic Insights

  • Simplicity sells complexity: Breaking down sophisticated financial concepts into visual narrative increased accessibility and credibility
  • Trust through aesthetics: Professional, minimal design positioning reinforced governmental authority and financial security
  • Multi-channel consistency: Unified messaging across broadcast, digital, and print maximized reach without message dilution
  • Storytelling over specs: Focus on benefits and outcomes (security, growth, national participation) outperformed technical financial details
  • Performance validates strategy: 1B SAR response demonstrated direct correlation between strategic creative direction and business outcomes
