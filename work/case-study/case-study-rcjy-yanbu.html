Challenge

The RCJY Yanbu Waterfront project represented one of the largest infrastructure development initiatives in Saudi Arabia—an 11-kilometer coastal development designed to position Yanbu as a premier international destination. The challenge was to communicate the scale, ambition, and commercial opportunity of this transformational project to both domestic and international stakeholders.

Key complexities:

  • Articulating project scale (SAR 380B investment across multiple phases)
  • Positioning coastal development as both commercial hub and cultural destination
  • Communicating infrastructure complexity to diverse audiences
  • Building stakeholder confidence in long-term vision
  • Creating cohesive narrative across 11km waterfront

Solution

Strategic Positioning: Developed comprehensive visual identity and messaging strategy that positioned Yanbu Waterfront as the nexus of commercial opportunity, cultural exchange, and international tourism. The approach emphasized the project's role in Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 diversification strategy.

Creative Execution: Created an integrated communications framework that included:

  • Master brand identity system for waterfront development
  • Environmental graphics and wayfinding systems
  • Investor presentation materials (English & Arabic)
  • Tourism & hospitality positioning collateral
  • Architectural visualization & concept renders
  • Multi-phase rollout strategy for 11km coastline
  • Stakeholder communication & engagement materials

Narrative Development: Positioned the waterfront as a transformational infrastructure project that bridged commercial development with cultural preservation, creating a compelling story for international investors, tourists, and local communities.

Key Strategic Insight: Rather than simply showcasing an infrastructure project, the positioning framed Yanbu Waterfront as a destination ecosystem—combining retail, hospitality, culture, and commerce into a unified narrative. This required visual systems that could adapt across multiple touchpoints while maintaining consistent positioning.

Strategic Significance

The RCJY Yanbu Waterfront project exemplified large-scale infrastructure positioning—translating a SAR 380B investment into compelling narratives for multiple audiences. The work demonstrated the ability to manage visual systems across massive geographic scope while maintaining strategic coherence and commercial clarity.

This project reinforced a core principle: ambitious infrastructure requires ambitious creative systems. Rather than treating the waterfront as a static architectural project, the positioning framed it as a dynamic ecosystem, enabling flexibility for future development while maintaining unified brand identity.
